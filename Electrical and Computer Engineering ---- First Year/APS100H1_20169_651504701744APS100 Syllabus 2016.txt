APS100H1F: ORIENTATION TO ENGINEERING - COURSE SYLLABUS FOR FALL 2016 

COURSE 
OBJECTIVES 

This course is designed to help students transition into first-year engineering studies and to develop and 
apply a greater understanding of the academic learning environment, the field of engineering, and how the 
fundamental mathematics and sciences are used in an engineering context.  Topics covered include: study 
skills, time management, problem solving, successful teamwork, effective communications, exam 
preparation, stress management and wellness, undergraduate research, extra- and co-curricular 
involvement, engineering disciplines and career opportunities, and applications of math and science in 
engineering.

SUGGESTED 
READINGS 

There is no required textbook for this course.  You may find these books to be useful references for this 
course and your other first-year courses. 

1)  Krista Donaldson, The Engineering Student Survival Guide, 3rd Ed., Boston, MA: McGraw-Hill, 

2005 

2)  Elizabeth A. Stephan, et. al., Thinking Like an Engineer, 3rd Ed., Boston, MA: Pearson, 2015 
3) Thomas R. Klassen and John A. Dwyer, How to Succeed at University (and Get a Great Job!), 

Vancouver, BC, UBC Press, free ebook available here.  

4) Cal Newport, How to Become a Straight-A Student, New York, NY: Three Rivers Press, 2006 
The first book is available for short-term loan from the Engineering Library and the First-Year 
Office (GB170) has a few of copies of this book also available for short-term loan. 

COURSE 
MARKS  

Final Test - 30%, Term Work - 25%, Tutorial Engagement - 24%, In-Class Questions (Top Hat) - 12%, 
Online Discussion – 5%, DELNA Quiz – 2%, Student Assessment Survey – 2%, Max. Total: 100%.  Final 
course grade will be based on the following system: Fail (F) 0% - 59%, Pass (P) 60% - 80%, High Pass (H) 
80% - 100%.  To pass the course, you must achieve a passing grade (≥50%) on the Final Test.  

FINAL TEST  

  There will be a final test for the course on Tuesday, December 6th from 1-2 PM.     
  The test will cover material related to engineering ethics and professional practice, fundamentals of 

engineering problem solving and time mapping, and will ask you to reflect on some of the 
experiences you have had in the classes and/or tutorials.
